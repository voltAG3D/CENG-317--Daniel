Things i wish i knew before starting this project, the floating 3.3vs on the pi and the fact the PI is not very good at controling PWM compared to other boards. Steps that i did to create the arm you will need 4-5 mirco analog feedback servos , male to male wires and female to female wires. 8 1k and 8 10k resistor for a pull down and 8 buttons as well as the 3d printed parts.

The button than moves the motor left and right
  • 3: Once the wiring is done here is some of mine it isn't the best. for the buttons i used the 3v volts from the pi aka pin 1 and for the motors the 5v pin which is pin 2. The ground is pin 6 and i used a breadboard to wire mine up which may not look nice and to stop the floating 3.3 volts you can use either a pull down or up resistor i did the pull down. before you test the motor make sure you have the correct wires in as the mirco servo ha 4 wires 3 are together and 1 white wire you may ignore this one. The other three must be done as follows brightest wire is the control or signal wire than the middle is power and the darkest is ground. Once you set that up test each motor and make sure they work.
